ADVICE

I do a considerable amount of research and deliberation before I buy my equipment. Often I deliberate far too long and new models or better options become available. So far, I've been pleased with the choices I've made.

I'm currently contemplating how to house my D-SLR camera (A Canon 450D - Rebel XSi). The guys at Ocean Optics in Essex have been really enthusiastic and helpful. There's also the cameras underwater website which i use for advice and from whom I bought my existing kit set-up.

I bought my first camera with an aim to use it underwater and the Ikelite housing seemed a decent choice. I went for a powerful strobe and because there was no TTL edition available, I bought an EV controller too.

So other's might benefit from my hindsight, here are just a few points to remember:
- Consider the size and weight of the housing and therefore your ability to transport the kit. Airlines seem to allow only 20KG in the hold nowadays and sometimes only 7KG in the cabin. The empty travel case for my camera weighs approx 5KG!
- Double-check the way you've fitted the camera inside the housing before you go diving!". On several occasions, the hot-shoe connection has slipped out, at least once I've fitted the camera with the shutter release lever in the wrong position and when i first got the camera, i didnt screw it to the baseplate firmly enough and the camera lens didnt extend properly!

-Consider buying two smaller strobes rather than a single big one. The strobe i currently have is heavy and cumbersome, especially out of the water. This is the only downside to it though - it's a great strobe!

-Arms with a large screw-lock mechanism are a pain! Especially if you want to change the lighting angle several times whilst photographing the same thing. Consider more easily adjusted arms. These locking arms only move in one direction too so you'll probably need to unlock BOTH ends to position the light perfectly for you. Bear in mind that what locks the arm under water, is unlikely to keep it locked above the water so no point locking the lights in position out of the water!

For the future, I'm considering an aluminum housing, multiple strobes (possibly slave strobes too) and an easily adjustable arm system. Several people I've spoken to have extolled the virtues of Subal housings and amongst these respected people are Alex Mustard (www.amustard.com) and Keith Lyall (www.kclyall.co.uk).
