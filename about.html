When I learnt to dive in 1997, I felt a new world opening up for me. I loved everything about it:- The colours, the movement, the abundance of life, the ability to float above a beautiful reef and watch the fish.

The first time I took an underwater photo in 2003, I realised I could record memories of incredible, unique animals in their delicate, natural environment. I could then share my experiences with friends and family and anyone who would spare the time.

Underwater photography has become such a passion over the years that a camera is now a standard part of my dive kit!
I truly believe that photography and conservation go hand in hand... I need to help preserve what can be experienced underwater in order to give myself the opportunity to take photographs of it! As such, I'm an ardent supporter of conservation projects and I became a diving instructor with the intention of teaching other people the skills to respect and care for the environment that I love so much.
More recently, I've started taking photos above the water too - primarily macro and landscape - I beleive what I learn by taking photos above water, I can transfer to my underwater work but I'm becoming more and more enthusiastic about the beauty in all types of photography. My aim is to put the underwater realm on show, promote a sense of integration and involvement and hopefully instill a desire to protect the marine environment and life underwater.
